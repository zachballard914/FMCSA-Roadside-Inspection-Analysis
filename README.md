## Project Overview

After spending more than 20 years in the trucking industry as both a CDL driver and an Operations Manager, I wanted to use
data analytics to better understand where roadside inspection violations occur and which violations are most likely to
place a driver or vehicle Out-of-Service (OOS).

Using FMCSA roadside inspection data from calendar years 2022-2026, I cleaned, analyzed, and visualized more than 23 million
inspection violations to identify trends that can help carriers improve safety, reduce preventable violations, and
prioritize driver training and preventative maintenance.

This was my first end-to-end data analytics project. I cleaned and analyzed the data in SQL and built an interactive
Tableau dashboard to communicate the results.

## Business Problem

Roadside inspections play an important role in keeping commercial vehicles and the motoring public safe. Most Out-of-
Service violations represent problems that could have been identified before a truck was pulled in for an inspection.
Understanding where the majority of these violations occur can help carriers focus maintenance and training efforts where 
they will have the greatest impact. 

For carriers, these violations increase costs, delay freight, disrupt operations, and negatively impact safety performance.
Understanding where they occur most often helps fleet managers focus maintenance and training where it will have the
greatest impact.

Throughout my career, I've learned that being proactive is always less costly than reacting after a violation occurs.

## Business Questions

This project was designed to answer four questions:

1. Where do the majority of roadside inspection violations occur?

2. Which violations most often result in an Out-of-Service (OOS) order?

3. Where should carriers focus their maintenance and inspection efforts?

4. What practical steps can carriers take to reduce preventable violations?

## Data Source & Scope

The data used in this project comes from the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) Roadside Inspection 
Violations report.

The analysis uses the FMCSA National Roadside Inspection report configured for calendar years 2022–2026 and includes more
than 23 million recorded violations across vehicle, driver, hazardous materials, and other inspection categories.

The project focuses on identifying the most common violations, the violations most likely to result in an Out-of-Service 
(OOS) order, and opportunities for preventative action.

This analysis uses the FMCSA Roadside Inspection Violations report with the following configuration:

- Report Focus: National
- Calendar Years: 2022–2026
- Domicile: All Domiciles
- Vehicle Type: All Vehicle Types
- Violation Type: All Types

## Recommendations

- Increase emphasis on tire inspection during pre-trip and post-trip inspections.

- Reinforce preventive maintenance programs focused on common roadside failures.

- Implement recurring driver retraining instead of one-time onboarding instruction.

- Use inspection trends to prioritize maintenance resources where they will have the greatest operational impact.
